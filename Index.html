Angela Davis

Angela Davis was born on January 26, 1944, and is a political activist, academic, and author who was heavily active in the American civil rights struggle. Her efforts and effect on racial justice, women's rights, and criminal justice reform are well recognized. Davis is aÂ professor in the History of Consciousness Department at the University of California, Santa Cruz, and the former director of the university's Feminist Studies Department. Davis was well-known in the 1960s and 1970s for her connections to the Black Panther Party. She was even on the Federal Bureau of Investigation's "Ten Most Wanted" list for a period. Davis co-founded Critical Resistance in 1997, an organization dedicated to the abolition of prisons, or what Davis and others refer to as the prison-industrial complex.

Malala Yousafzai

Malala at a young age spoke out about the right to education for women. She created the Malala fund to bring awareness to social and economic issues for women's education. She was focused on improving the lives of other girls to make it easier for them to get an education. Malala is from Pakistan and in Pakistan the Taliban wanted to prevent girls from going to school. Malala stood up to the Taliban and spoke out to earn her right to an education. The Taliban attacked Malala for speaking out and shot her in the head. Although they did all of this to her she continued pushing through and fought for equality. She gained recognition and became the youngest to earn the Nobel peace prize at 17.
